Helena Price

London Bureau Chief

Beats: Europe, diplomacy, markets, energy, NATO, U.K. politics, transatlantic affairs

Helena Price is the London Bureau Chief for CGN News, directing coverage of Europe, diplomacy, markets, energy, NATO, U.K. politics and transatlantic affairs. Her work connects Westminster, Brussels, financial markets and international security debates to the practical consequences facing readers, businesses and governments. Price brings a composed foreign-affairs voice to stories involving alliances, trade, defense, sanctions, energy security and the political pressures shaping Europe.

Priya Ashford

Local/Civic Reporter

Beats: Local / civic affairs

Born in Manchester and educated at King's College London, Priya Ashford grew up in a family that treated local council meetings, bus routes and school budgets as dinner-table news. She covers local and civic affairs for CGN London, with a close eye on city government, transport, public services and the everyday decisions that shape neighbourhood life. Priya reports in English and French, keeps a rescue terrier named Mabel, and is known in the bureau for mapping stories by walking the route first.

london@cgnnews.net

Thomas Hale

Politics Reporter

Beats: Politics

Born in Bristol and educated at the University of Edinburgh, Thomas Hale came to politics reporting after volunteering on a student election desk and discovering that procedure often explains power. He covers government, elections, policy and public institutions for CGN London, connecting Westminster debates to the choices facing voters, families and businesses. Thomas reports in English and German, follows lower-league football, and keeps a battered notebook he calls Parliament Junior.

london@cgnnews.net

Charlotte Ward

Business & Markets Reporter

Beats: Business / markets

Born in London and educated at the London School of Economics, Charlotte Ward grew up above her parents' small shop and learned early how national policy can land first on local tills. She covers business, markets, trade and the consumer economy for CGN London, following the links between financial centres, supply chains, employers and household budgets. Charlotte reports in English and Portuguese, has a cat named Sterling, and makes a point of reading market reports with coffee strong enough to count as a second deadline.

london@cgnnews.net

Grace Whitmore

Meteorologist

Born in Norwich and educated at the University of Reading, Grace Whitmore became interested in weather after childhood flood warnings turned forecasts into something her family planned around, not background noise. Born in Birmingham and educated at Loughborough University, Henry Shaw grew up around Saturday fixtures, crowded trains and the feeling that sport tells you as much about a place as its politics. Nadia Clarke
